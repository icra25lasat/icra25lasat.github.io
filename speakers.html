Confirmed Plenary Speakers @Chile

Jan Peters
German Research Center for AI (DFKI), Research Department: SAIROL
Hessian Centre for Artificial Intelligence

Martin Adams
Department of Electrical Engineering
Universidad de Chile
Title of the talk: Advancing State Estimation: Insights from Random Finite Sets

This talk will explore recent advancements in state estimation techniques, focusing on the application of random finite sets theory. We'll discuss how this approach provides a more robust framework for handling uncertainty in dynamic systems, particularly in robotics applications. The presentation will cover both theoretical foundations and practical implementations, demonstrating improved performance in real-world scenarios compared to traditional methods.

Darius Burschka
Robotics, Artificial Intelligence and Embedded Systems
Technical University of Munich
Title of the talk: Robust and Efficient Coupling of Perception to Actuation in Dynamic Environments

I will discuss the problem of efficient and robust information exchange between the perception and actuation modules in manipulation and mobile systems. While most current approaches use three-dimensional representations of the world as an interface to generate actions in a robot, this is not the native representation neither of the sensor nor of the motion controller and it requires calibration parameters to calculate. It is prone to errors and drifts making the accuracy of the system unreliable. Direct definitions of the tasks in the sensor space or some lower dimensional abstraction allows a robust operations of the systems in dynamic environments. I will show examples of non-metric task representation for navigation, path planning, obstacle avoidance and manipulation.

Paulo Drews-Jr
Robot Learning Lab
Albert-Ludwigs-Universität Freiburg

In this talk, we'll explore cutting-edge approaches to robot learning in unstructured environments. We'll examine how modern machine learning techniques can be combined with traditional robotics methods to create systems that can adapt to novel situations while maintaining safety and reliability. The presentation will include case studies from underwater robotics and autonomous aerial systems, demonstrating practical applications of these concepts.

Streaming Plenary Speakers @ICRA 2025

Allison Okamura
Title of the talk: Rewired: The Interplay of Robots and Society

This talk will examine the evolving relationship between robotics technology and human society. We'll explore how robots are reshaping industries, healthcare, and daily life, while also considering the societal implications of these changes. The presentation will highlight both the opportunities and challenges that arise as robots become increasingly integrated into our world, with a focus on creating technologies that benefit all members of society.

Tessa Lau

In this presentation, we'll explore the future of human-robot collaboration in service and domestic environments. The talk will focus on how robots can be designed to better understand and adapt to human needs, creating more natural and effective interactions. We'll examine case studies from real-world deployments and discuss the technical challenges involved in creating robots that can operate reliably in unstructured human environments.

Raffaello D'Andrea

This talk will present innovative approaches to robotic system design that combine artistic creativity with engineering precision. We'll explore how principles from dynamic systems and control theory can be applied to create robotic systems that exhibit both functional reliability and aesthetic appeal. The presentation will include demonstrations of flying machines and other robotic artworks that challenge conventional notions of what robots can be and do.
